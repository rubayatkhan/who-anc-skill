### Diagnostic Reasoning
This is classified as **gestational hypertension** based on the following criteria:
1. **New-onset hypertension after 20 weeks GA**: BP >= 140/90 mmHg confirmed on two separate readings (148/95 and 146/94) at 32 weeks gestation
2. **Previously normotensive**: All blood pressure readings before 20 weeks were normal (112/68 at 12 weeks, 118/72 at 20 weeks), ruling out chronic hypertension
3. **No proteinuria**: Urine dipstick is protein-negative, which is the critical factor differentiating this from pre-eclampsia
4. **No severe features**: Absence of headache, visual changes, epigastric pain, or other signs of end-organ dysfunction — this is NOT pre-eclampsia with severe features
5. **BP is below 160/110 threshold**: This is not in the severe range

**This is NOT pre-eclampsia** — pre-eclampsia requires hypertension PLUS proteinuria (>= 300mg/24h or dipstick >= +1) and/or end-organ dysfunction. This patient has hypertension only.

**This is NOT chronic hypertension** — all BP readings prior to 20 weeks were documented as normal.

## Recommendations

### Hypertension Management
1. **Blood pressure monitoring — twice weekly**: Patient should return every 3-4 days for BP measurement, or sooner if any new symptoms develop
2. **Weekly proteinuria screening**: Urine dipstick for protein at every visit — serial monitoring is essential to detect progression to pre-eclampsia
3. **Consider antihypertensive medication**: Oral methyldopa (250 mg two to three times daily) may be initiated to maintain BP < 140/90 mmHg — decision per clinical officer/physician
4. **Baseline laboratory work**: CBC with platelets, liver transaminases, serum creatinine — these values will serve as comparison if the condition progresses to pre-eclampsia
5. **Fetal surveillance**: Growth ultrasound at 34-36 weeks given hypertension diagnosis
6. **Delivery planning**: Plan delivery at **37 weeks** if gestational hypertension remains stable without progression to pre-eclampsia. If BP becomes difficult to control or pre-eclampsia features develop, earlier delivery may be indicated.
