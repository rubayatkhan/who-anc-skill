## Recommendations

### GDM Screening — Priority Action This Contact
**A 75g oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT) is indicated and should be scheduled within 1-2 weeks.** This patient has multiple risk factors for gestational diabetes:
1. **Previous macrosomic infant** (4.2 kg birth weight in prior pregnancy)
2. **Family history of type 2 diabetes** (mother, first-degree relative)
3. **BMI 29 kg/m2** (overweight, approaching obese threshold)
4. **Elevated random blood glucose** (7.8 mmol/L) with trace glycosuria

The OGTT involves fasting, 1-hour, and 2-hour glucose measurements after a 75g oral glucose load.

**GDM diagnostic criteria (WHO 2013):**
- Fasting: 5.1-6.9 mmol/L
- 1-hour: 10.0 mmol/L or greater
- 2-hour: 8.5-11.0 mmol/L

**Diabetes in pregnancy criteria:**
- Fasting: 7.0 mmol/L or greater
- 2-hour: 11.1 mmol/L or greater

**If GDM is confirmed:**
- Medical nutrition therapy (dietary modification) as first-line management
- Moderate physical activity counseling
- Self-monitoring of blood glucose if resources allow
- Referral for specialist management if dietary measures insufficient
- More frequent ANC contacts for fetal and maternal monitoring
- Plan delivery at facility with capacity for managing GDM complications
