# ANC Guidance for Adolescent Patient

## Patient Overview
Blessing T. is a 16-year-old primigravida at approximately 8 weeks gestation by her last menstrual period, though dating is uncertain due to irregular cycles. This is her first ANC visit. She is accompanied by her mother.

## Key Findings

### Vital Signs and Labs
- Blood pressure is normal at 100/62 mmHg
- She is underweight with a BMI of 17.6 and weighs 45 kg at 160 cm tall
- Her MUAC is 21.5 cm, which suggests borderline risk for undernutrition
- Hemoglobin is 10.5 g/dL, indicating mild anemia
- HIV negative, syphilis non-reactive
- Blood group A Rh-positive
- Urine tests are normal

### Concerns
- Low body weight and poor nutritional status
- Mild anemia
- Positive depression screening (PHQ-2 score of 4)
- Adolescent with unplanned pregnancy, social concerns about school and stigma
- Uncertain gestational dating

## Recommendations

### Nutrition
Blessing's low BMI and poor dietary intake are concerning. She should receive dietary counseling focusing on eating more protein, iron-rich foods, fruits, and vegetables. Given household food insecurity, referral to any available food assistance programs would be helpful. Her weight should be monitored at each visit.

### Anemia
With a hemoglobin of 10.5, she has mild anemia. She should start iron and folic acid supplements. Iron-rich foods should be encouraged. Her hemoglobin should be rechecked in about a month.

### Vaccinations
Since there are no vaccination records available, she should begin tetanus vaccination. She should receive her first dose today and a second dose in about 4 weeks.

### Ultrasound
Given the uncertain dates and irregular cycles, she should be referred for an ultrasound to confirm how far along she is. This is important for planning her care.

### Mental Health
Her positive PHQ-2 screening is important to address. She should have further assessment of her mood, possibly with a more detailed questionnaire. Counseling support should be offered, ideally through adolescent-friendly services. Her mother can be involved as a support person if Blessing agrees.

### School and Social Support
Information about policies that allow pregnant students to continue their education should be provided. She may benefit from connection to community support groups for young mothers.

### General Pregnancy Counseling
She should be educated about warning signs in pregnancy that require immediate medical attention, such as bleeding, severe headache, or abdominal pain. Birth planning should begin, involving both Blessing and her mother.

### Follow-Up
- Refer for dating ultrasound as soon as possible
- Return for next ANC visit around 20 weeks
- Community health worker follow-up would be beneficial for nutrition monitoring and adherence

## Note
This guidance is general in nature. All clinical decisions should be made by qualified healthcare providers based on individual patient assessment.
