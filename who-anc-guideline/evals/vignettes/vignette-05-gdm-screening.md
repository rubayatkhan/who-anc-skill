## Expected Clinical Actions (Evaluation Key)

- **GDM screening is indicated.** Multiple risk factors:
  - Previous macrosomic infant (4.2 kg)
  - Family history of type 2 diabetes (first-degree relative)
  - BMI 29 (overweight, approaching obese)
  - Random blood glucose 7.8 mmol/L (elevated; trace glycosuria)
- **WHO recommendation:** Hyperglycemia in pregnancy should be diagnosed using a **75g OGTT** (fasting, 1-hour, and 2-hour glucose measurements)
  - GDM diagnostic criteria (WHO 2013): fasting 5.1-6.9 mmol/L, 1hr >=10.0 mmol/L, or 2hr 8.5-11.0 mmol/L
  - Diabetes in pregnancy: fasting >=7.0 mmol/L or 2hr >=11.1 mmol/L
- **If GDM confirmed:**
  - Dietary counseling (medical nutrition therapy) as first-line management
  - Moderate physical activity counseling
  - Self-monitoring of blood glucose if resources allow
  - Refer for specialist management if dietary measures insufficient
  - More frequent ANC contacts for fetal and maternal monitoring
  - Plan delivery at facility with capacity for managing GDM complications
- **Current contact actions regardless of OGTT result:**
  - Continue iron and folic acid supplementation
  - Dietary counseling: reduce refined sugars and sweetened beverages, increase fiber, balanced meals
  - Encourage regular moderate physical activity (walking 30 min/day)
  - Schedule OGTT within 1-2 weeks
  - Pre-eclampsia risk: low (no high-risk factors; BMI alone is a single moderate factor)
  - Schedule next ANC contact at 30 weeks (Contact 4)
