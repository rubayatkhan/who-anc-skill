## Expected Clinical Actions (Evaluation Key)

- **Anemia:** Mild anemia (Hb 10.8 g/dL); in a setting with >=40% anemia prevalence, provide therapeutic iron 120 mg elemental iron daily + folic acid 0.4 mg
- **ART:** Continue current TDF/3TC/DTG regimen; no change needed given viral suppression
- **Viral load monitoring:** Next viral load at 34-36 weeks (or per national protocol) to guide delivery planning
- **PMTCT plan:**
  - Continue ART throughout pregnancy, delivery, and postpartum
  - Plan for infant ARV prophylaxis at birth (per national PMTCT guidelines)
  - Counsel on safe infant feeding (exclusive breastfeeding with ART is WHO-recommended)
- **Cotrimoxazole:** NOT indicated at this time (CD4 >350, no clinical indication). If cotrimoxazole were started, IPTp-SP would be contraindicated due to sulfa drug interaction.
- **IPTp-SP:** Patient IS eligible for IPTp-SP (in malaria-endemic area, NOT on cotrimoxazole). Administer first dose of SP at this contact (>=13 weeks GA). Doses at each scheduled contact, at least 1 month apart.
- **TB screening:** Screen for TB symptoms (cough, fever, night sweats, weight loss) at every visit
- **Hepatitis B:** Already tested negative
- **Partner and family:** Continue couple counseling; partner ART adherence support
- **Delivery plan:** If viral load remains suppressed, vaginal delivery is appropriate
- **Postpartum:** Continue ART lifelong; plan infant HIV testing per national protocol (typically at 6 weeks by PCR)
