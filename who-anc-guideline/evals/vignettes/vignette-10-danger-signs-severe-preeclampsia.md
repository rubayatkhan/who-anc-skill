## Expected Evaluation Points
- **DANGER SIGNS — EMERGENCY CLASSIFICATION**
- **Diagnosis: PRE-ECLAMPSIA WITH SEVERE FEATURES** based on:
  - Blood pressure >= 160/110 mmHg (confirmed on two readings 15 minutes apart)
  - Significant proteinuria (+++ / 3+ on dipstick)
  - Severe headache (unresponsive to simple analgesia)
  - Visual disturbances (flashing lights, blurred vision)
  - Epigastric pain
  - New onset — all prior ANC contacts were normotensive with no proteinuria
- **IMMEDIATE MANAGEMENT:**
  1. **Magnesium sulfate (MgSO4):** Loading dose for seizure prophylaxis — 4g IV over 15-20 minutes PLUS 10g IM (5g in each buttock) OR institutional protocol. Continue maintenance dose 5g IM every 4 hours OR 1g/hr IV infusion for 24 hours
  2. **Antihypertensive therapy:** Urgent BP reduction — nifedipine 10mg oral (immediate release), OR hydralazine 5mg IV, OR labetalol 20mg IV. Target: BP <150/100 but not below 130/80
  3. **Antenatal corticosteroids:** Dexamethasone 6mg IM — first dose NOW (34 weeks GA, delivery anticipated within 7 days). Second dose 12 hours later. Total course: 4 doses of 6mg every 12 hours
  4. **IV access:** Establish with large-bore cannula
  5. **Fluid management:** Strict fluid balance, do NOT fluid overload — restrict to 80 mL/hour total
  6. **Urinary catheter:** Insert for strict monitoring of urine output (target >= 30 mL/hour)
  7. **Laboratory investigations:** URGENT — CBC (platelet count), liver function tests (AST/ALT), serum creatinine, uric acid, coagulation studies (PT/INR) to assess for HELLP syndrome or end-organ damage
  8. **Fetal monitoring:** Continuous CTG if available, or frequent intermittent auscultation
  9. **Call obstetrician:** Notify on-call obstetrician IMMEDIATELY for delivery planning
  10. **Delivery planning:** At 34 weeks with severe pre-eclampsia, delivery is the definitive treatment — plan delivery after maternal stabilization (within 24-48 hours, or sooner if maternal or fetal condition deteriorates)
- **MONITORING during stabilization:**
  - BP every 15 minutes until stable, then every 30 minutes
  - Urine output hourly
  - Deep tendon reflexes and respiratory rate every 1 hour (MgSO4 toxicity monitoring)
  - Fetal heart rate every 15-30 minutes or continuous CTG
  - Assess for signs of progression: worsening headache, visual loss, seizure (eclampsia), right upper quadrant pain, HELLP syndrome
- **This is NOT a case for outpatient management** — patient must not be sent home
- **If facility cannot manage:** Stabilize with MgSO4 loading dose and first antihypertensive, then URGENT transfer to higher-level facility with obstetric surgery and neonatal intensive care capability
