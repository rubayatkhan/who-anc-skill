## Expected Evaluation Points
- **Moderate anemia (Hb 8.5 g/dL):** Declining trend (10.8 → 9.6 → 8.5) despite prescribed supplementation — switch from prophylactic to THERAPEUTIC iron dosing (120 mg elemental iron daily), counsel on adherence and side-effect management, dietary counseling, recheck Hb in 4 weeks, investigate cause if no response
- **Pre-eclampsia risk — HIGH:** Multiple risk factors present:
  - Nulliparity
  - BMI ≥30
  - New proteinuria (+1)
  - Elevated BP (130/85, trending upward)
  - Family history of pre-eclampsia (sister)
  - Age ≥35 approaching
- **Low-dose aspirin (75-150 mg daily):** Should have been started before 20 weeks — initiate NOW if not yet started, though benefit is reduced when started after 20 weeks
- **Calcium supplementation:** Ensure adequate dose (1.5-2.0 g elemental calcium daily in divided doses) — reassess adherence
- **Proteinuria monitoring:** +1 protein on dipstick — requires close follow-up; repeat at each visit; if persistent or worsening, quantify (24-hour urine or protein/creatinine ratio if available)
- **Blood pressure:** 130/85 is elevated but below threshold for gestational hypertension (140/90); however, with proteinuria and risk factors, warrants close monitoring — recheck in 1-2 weeks
- **Random glucose 6.2 mmol/L:** Non-fasting value is not diagnostic; however, given obesity and family history of diabetes, formal GDM screening with 75g OGTT should be performed at 24-28 weeks (due NOW at 24 weeks)
- **Prior myomectomy with cavity entry:** Classify as having a uterine scar — discuss delivery planning with obstetric team; may require planned cesarean depending on scar assessment and facility capability
- **Facility delivery mandatory:** Given multiple risk factors, delivery must occur at a facility with cesarean section and blood transfusion capability
- **Increased visit frequency:** Consider more frequent BP and proteinuria monitoring given risk profile
- **Danger sign counseling:** Emphasize headache, visual changes, epigastric pain, sudden swelling, decreased fetal movement
