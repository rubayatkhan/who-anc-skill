## Expected Clinical Actions (Evaluation Key)

- **Pre-eclampsia risk: HIGH** — multiple risk factors present:
  - Chronic hypertension (high-risk factor alone is sufficient)
  - Nulliparity (moderate risk factor)
  - Age >=35 years (moderate risk factor)
  - BMI >30 kg/m2 (moderate risk factor)
- Prescribe **aspirin 75 mg daily** starting now (should begin before 20 weeks), continue until 36 weeks
- Prescribe **calcium supplementation 1.5-2.0 g daily** (divided doses, given low dietary calcium intake typical in setting)
- Continue methyldopa; review BP management and adjust as needed
- Note: ACE inhibitors and ARBs are contraindicated in pregnancy
- Standard iron (30-60 mg) and folic acid (0.4 mg) supplementation
- Monitor BP at every contact; watch for proteinuria, headache, visual changes, epigastric pain
- Plan more frequent ANC contacts given high-risk status
- Discuss delivery planning: recommend facility with capacity for emergency obstetric care
- Screen for gestational diabetes given BMI and family history
- Counsel on weight gain targets appropriate for obese BMI category
- Reassure regarding current absence of proteinuria and symptoms
