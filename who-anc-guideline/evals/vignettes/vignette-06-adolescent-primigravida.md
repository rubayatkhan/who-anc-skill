## Expected Clinical Actions (Evaluation Key)

### Nutritional Priority
- **Underweight (BMI 17.6):** Nutritional assessment and counseling is critical
- Balanced energy and protein supplementation recommended (WHO recommends in undernourished populations)
- Dietary counseling: diverse diet, increased protein, iron-rich foods, fruits, and vegetables
- Consider food supplementation program referral if available
- Monitor weight gain closely at every contact

### Anemia Management
- Mild anemia (Hb 10.5 g/dL) in a high-prevalence setting (>=40%)
- Therapeutic iron: **120 mg elemental iron daily** + folic acid 0.4 mg
- Deworming: single-dose albendazole after first trimester
- Recheck hemoglobin in 4 weeks

### Vaccination
- No vaccination records available
- Initiate **tetanus toxoid vaccination** schedule: TT1 at this visit, TT2 at least 4 weeks later
- Document and provide new vaccination card

### Dating and Ultrasound
- Uncertain LMP with irregular cycles: **refer for dating ultrasound** as soon as possible
- Accurate gestational age is essential for care planning

### Psychosocial Support
- PHQ-2 positive: further assessment with PHQ-9 recommended
- Provide supportive counseling; consider referral to psychosocial support services or adolescent-friendly mental health services
- Assess school continuation plans; provide information on policies supporting pregnant students
- Engage mother as support person (with patient's consent)
- Offer information on social services and community support groups for adolescent mothers

### Age-Appropriate Counseling
- Use adolescent-friendly communication approaches
- Counsel on danger signs in pregnancy using age-appropriate language
- Discuss birth preparedness and complication readiness with both patient and mother
- Address contraception counseling for postpartum period
- Ensure confidentiality while maintaining age-appropriate parental/guardian involvement

### Pre-eclampsia Risk
- Primigravidity is a moderate risk factor
- Age alone (adolescent) is not a standard WHO high-risk factor for pre-eclampsia
- One moderate risk factor does not meet the threshold for aspirin prophylaxis
- Monitor BP at each contact

### Follow-Up Plan
- Schedule more frequent contacts if possible given adolescent and nutritional risk
- Ensure referral for ultrasound dating within 1-2 weeks
- Next ANC contact at approximately 20 weeks (Contact 2)
- Community health worker follow-up for adherence support and nutrition monitoring
