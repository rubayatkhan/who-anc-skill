# Counseling Topics — Gestational Age Mapped

## First Trimester (Contacts 1)

### Nutrition & Supplements
- Balanced diet with emphasis on iron-rich foods (dark leafy greens, beans, meat)
- Importance of iron and folic acid supplementation — daily adherence
- Foods that enhance iron absorption (citrus, tomatoes) vs inhibit (tea, coffee with meals)
- Calcium-rich foods (dairy, sardines with bones, dark greens) and supplement timing
- Adequate hydration (8-10 glasses daily)
- Avoid alcohol, tobacco, and recreational drugs

### Danger Sign Recognition
- Teach the 12 danger signs (see danger-signs.md)
- When and where to seek emergency care
- Importance of having a companion who also knows the signs

### Infection Prevention
- Handwashing, food safety, safe water
- Malaria prevention: ITN use every night (endemic areas)
- Avoidance of cat litter, undercooked meat (toxoplasmosis)
- STI prevention

### Emotional Wellbeing
- Normal emotional changes in pregnancy
- Screen for depression/anxiety (PHQ-2 or equivalent)
- Support systems and partner involvement
- Adolescent-specific: school continuation, social support, peer groups

## Second Trimester (Contacts 2-3)

### Fetal Development & Monitoring
- Expected fetal movements (quickening ~16-20 weeks)
- Importance of reporting decreased fetal movement
- Understanding ultrasound findings (if applicable)

### Birth Preparedness (Introduce)
- Identify skilled birth attendant and facility
- Identify transport to facility
- Save money for transport and supplies
- Identify compatible blood donor
- Identify decision-maker in emergency

### GDM & Nutritional Counseling (Contact 3)
- Healthy eating to prevent/manage gestational diabetes
- Warning signs of hyperglycemia
- Importance of glucose screening at 24-28 weeks

### Family Planning Awareness
- Introduce concept of healthy birth spacing (≥2 years)
- Overview of postpartum contraception options
- Partner involvement in family planning discussion

### Malaria Prevention Reinforcement (Endemic Areas)
- ITN use nightly — entire household
- IPTp-SP schedule — importance of taking at clinic visits
- Seek care immediately for fever

## Third Trimester (Contacts 4-8)

### Birth Preparedness (Detailed)
- Finalize birth plan: facility, transport, emergency contacts
- Pack a delivery bag: clean cloths, razor blade, cord ties, pads
- Know signs of labor onset vs false labor
- When to go to the facility (regular painful contractions, water breaking, bleeding)

### Breastfeeding (Contacts 5-6)
- Exclusive breastfeeding for first 6 months
- Initiation within 1 hour of birth (skin-to-skin)
- Colostrum — importance as "first immunization"
- Positioning and attachment basics
- Common challenges and where to get support
- HIV-positive: exclusive breastfeeding still recommended with ART

### Newborn Care (Contacts 6-7)
- Thermal care: skin-to-skin, delayed bathing (24 hours)
- Cord care: clean, dry, no application of substances
- Danger signs in newborn: not feeding, fever, convulsions, jaundice, fast breathing
- Immunization schedule (BCG, OPV-0 at birth)
- Birth registration

### Postpartum Planning (Contacts 7-8)
- Postpartum danger signs (heavy bleeding, fever, foul discharge)
- Postpartum visit schedule (within 24h, 48-72h, 7-14 days, 6 weeks)
- Postpartum family planning — method selection
- Return to fertility after delivery
- Emotional wellbeing postpartum — signs of postpartum depression

### Danger Sign Review (Every Contact)
- Reinforce danger signs at EVERY contact
- Test understanding: ask woman and companion to list signs
- Update emergency plan as needed
- Specific danger signs for current GA (e.g., preterm labor signs from 28w onward)
