# Danger Signs in Pregnancy — Immediate Referral Triggers

## EMERGENCY — Act Within Minutes

| Danger Sign | Likely Diagnosis | Immediate Action |
|------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Seizure / convulsion | Eclampsia | MgSO4 loading dose, left lateral, protect airway, REFER |
| Unconscious / unresponsive | Eclampsia, cerebral malaria, shock | Stabilize airway, IV access, REFER immediately |
| BP ≥ 160/110 with symptoms | Severe pre-eclampsia | MgSO4 + antihypertensive, REFER |
| Heavy vaginal bleeding | Placental abruption, placenta previa | IV fluids, do NOT examine vaginally, REFER |
| Severe abdominal pain (non-labor) | Abruption, rupture, ectopic | IV access, assess for shock, REFER |
| Severe difficulty breathing | Pulmonary edema, PE, severe anemia | Sit upright, oxygen if available, REFER |
| Fever > 39.5°C with rigors | Severe malaria, sepsis | Blood smear, IV antimalarials/antibiotics, REFER |
| Cord prolapse | Obstetric emergency | Knee-chest position, do NOT push cord back, REFER for cesarean |

## URGENT — Act Within Hours

| Danger Sign | Likely Diagnosis | Action |
|------------|-----------------|--------|
| BP 140-159/90-109 with headache | Pre-eclampsia evolving | Assess proteinuria, labs, consider MgSO4 prophylaxis, plan referral |
| Persistent severe headache | Pre-eclampsia, meningitis | BP check, proteinuria, fundoscopy if available |
| Visual disturbances | Pre-eclampsia with severe features | BP + proteinuria + assess for severe features |
| Epigastric / RUQ pain | Pre-eclampsia (HELLP), hepatic | BP + proteinuria + liver enzymes if available |
| Reduced / absent fetal movement | Fetal compromise | Fetal heart rate assessment, NST if available, consider delivery |
| Fever > 38°C without obvious source | Malaria, UTI, chorioamnionitis | Blood smear, urine culture, assess for PROM |
| Painful contractions < 37 weeks | Preterm labor | Assess cervical change, tocolysis if appropriate, corticosteroids, REFER |
| Rupture of membranes < 37 weeks | PPROM | Confirm, antibiotics, corticosteroids if 24-34w, REFER |
| Severe pallor / weakness | Severe anemia | Check Hb urgently, consider transfusion, investigate cause |
| Foul-smelling vaginal discharge | Infection, PROM with infection | Assess for chorioamnionitis, antibiotics, REFER |
| Persistent vomiting (unable to keep fluids) | Hyperemesis, hepatitis, pre-eclampsia | IV fluids, assess for ketonuria, liver function |

## Counseling All Women on Danger Signs

At **every ANC contact**, counsel the woman and her companion to seek care IMMEDIATELY if:

1. Vaginal bleeding (any amount)
2. Severe headache not relieved by paracetamol
3. Vision problems (blurring, seeing spots)
4. Swelling of face or hands (sudden onset)
5. Convulsions / fits
6. Loss of consciousness
7. Difficulty breathing
8. Fever / feeling very hot
9. Severe abdominal pain
10. Baby moving less or not at all
11. Water breaking before labor
12. Feeling very weak or tired (severe pallor)

## Eclampsia-Specific Danger Signs
- Premonitory symptoms: severe headache, visual changes, hyperreflexia, epigastric pain
- These may precede seizure by hours — treat as severe pre-eclampsia

## Severe Malaria-Specific Danger Signs
- Altered consciousness (cerebral malaria)
- Prostration (unable to sit or stand)
- Multiple convulsions
- Respiratory distress (acidotic breathing)
- Severe jaundice
- Dark urine (hemoglobinuria / blackwater fever)
