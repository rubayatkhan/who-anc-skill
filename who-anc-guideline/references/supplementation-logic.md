# Supplementation Logic — WHO ANC Guidelines

## Iron Supplementation

### Population-Level Decision

| Anemia Prevalence | Recommendation |
|-------------------|---------------|
| ≥ 40% (high) | Daily oral iron for ALL pregnant women |
| 20-39% (moderate) | Daily oral iron for ALL pregnant women |
| < 20% (low) | Intermittent iron (120mg weekly) OR daily if individual Hb low |

### Individual Dosing

| Hb Level | Classification | Iron Dose | Folic Acid | Follow-up |
|----------|---------------|-----------|------------|-----------|
| ≥ 11.0 g/dL | Normal | 30-60mg elemental iron daily | 400mcg daily | Recheck Hb at Contact 3, 6 |
| 10.0-10.9 g/dL | Mild anemia | 30-60mg elemental iron daily | 400mcg daily | Recheck Hb at next contact |
| 7.0-9.9 g/dL | Moderate anemia | 120mg elemental iron daily | 400mcg daily | Recheck Hb in 1-3 months |
| < 7.0 g/dL | Severe anemia | 120mg elemental iron daily + REFER | 400mcg daily | Recheck in 2 weeks, consider IV iron/transfusion |

### Iron Formulations (Elemental Iron Content)

| Formulation | Tablet Strength | Elemental Iron |
|------------|----------------|----------------|
| Ferrous sulfate | 300mg | 60mg |
| Ferrous fumarate | 200mg | 66mg |
| Ferrous gluconate | 300mg | 35mg |

### Counseling Points for Iron
- Take on empty stomach or with vitamin C for better absorption
- Avoid taking with tea, coffee, or calcium supplements (reduce absorption by 50-60%)
- Common side effects: constipation, dark stools, nausea
- If GI intolerance: take with food (reduced absorption but better adherence)
- Take iron and calcium supplements at least 2 hours apart

## Folic Acid

| Timing | Dose | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| Pre-conception (if possible) | 400mcg daily | Ideally start 3 months before conception |
| Throughout pregnancy | 400mcg daily | Neural tube defect prevention (most critical in first 28 days) |
| History of NTD-affected pregnancy | 4-5mg daily | Higher dose for recurrence prevention |

## Calcium

### Population-Level Decision

| Dietary Calcium Intake | Recommendation |
|-----------------------|---------------|
| Low intake (< 600mg/day) | 1.5-2g daily for ALL pregnant women |
| Adequate intake (≥ 600mg/day) | Only if elevated pre-eclampsia risk |

### Dosing

| Indication | Dose | Timing | Duration |
|-----------|------|--------|----------|
| Low-intake population | 1.5-2g daily | Divided into 3 doses with meals | Throughout pregnancy |
| High PE risk | 1.5-2g daily | Divided into 3 doses with meals | Throughout pregnancy |

### Key Rules
- Divide total daily dose into 3 doses (breakfast, lunch, dinner)
- Take at least 2 hours apart from iron supplements
- Calcium carbonate: take with food (needs stomach acid for absorption)
- Calcium citrate: can take with or without food

## Aspirin (for Pre-eclampsia Prevention)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Dose | 75mg daily (WHO standard) |
| Start | Ideally at 12 weeks, acceptable up to 20 weeks |
| Stop | At 36 weeks gestation |
| Indication | HIGH risk (≥1 high factor) OR MODERATE risk (≥2 moderate factors) |
| Timing | Take at bedtime (evidence suggests better efficacy) |
| Contraindications | Active bleeding, platelet disorder, aspirin allergy, peptic ulcer disease |
