# Clinical Decision Trees — WHO ANC Guidelines

## 1. Iron Supplementation Decision Tree

```
STEP 1: Determine population anemia prevalence
  IF prevalence ≥ 40% (high):
    → ALL pregnant women get daily iron supplementation
    → Proceed to Step 2 for dosing
  IF prevalence 20-39% (moderate):
    → ALL pregnant women get daily iron supplementation
    → Proceed to Step 2 for dosing
  IF prevalence < 20% (low):
    → Intermittent iron (120mg weekly) is an option
    → OR daily iron if individual Hb is low

STEP 2: Check individual hemoglobin
  IF Hb ≥ 11.0 g/dL (normal):
    → Prophylactic: 30-60mg elemental iron daily
    → PLUS folic acid 400mcg daily
  IF Hb 10.0-10.9 g/dL (mild anemia):
    → Prophylactic: 30-60mg elemental iron daily
    → PLUS folic acid 400mcg daily
    → Recheck Hb at next contact
  IF Hb 7.0-9.9 g/dL (moderate anemia):
    → Therapeutic: 120mg elemental iron daily
    → PLUS folic acid 400mcg daily
    → Recheck Hb in 1-3 months
    → Add dietary counseling (iron-rich foods, vitamin C, avoid tea with meals)
  IF Hb < 7.0 g/dL (severe anemia):
    → REFER to higher-level facility
    → Therapeutic: 120mg elemental iron daily
    → Consider IV iron or blood transfusion
    → Investigate cause (malaria, hookworm, etc.)
    → Recheck Hb in 2 weeks

STEP 3: Follow-up
  IF on therapeutic dose AND Hb rises ≥ 1 g/dL in 1 month:
    → Continue therapeutic dose until Hb ≥ 11.0
    → Then switch to prophylactic dose
  IF no improvement after 1 month of therapeutic dose:
    → Investigate: adherence, malaria, helminth infection, other causes
    → Consider parenteral iron
```

## 2. Calcium Supplementation Decision Tree

```
STEP 1: Assess dietary calcium intake
  IF population has LOW dietary calcium intake (<600mg/day):
    → Calcium supplementation recommended for ALL pregnant women
    → Dose: 1.5-2g daily, divided into 3 doses
    → Take separately from iron (≥2 hours apart)
  IF population has ADEQUATE calcium intake:
    → Supplementation NOT routinely needed
    → UNLESS individual is at elevated pre-eclampsia risk

STEP 2: Check pre-eclampsia risk
  IF HIGH risk (≥1 high-risk factor) OR MODERATE risk (≥2 moderate factors):
    → Calcium 1.5-2g daily recommended regardless of population intake
    → Combine with aspirin 75mg daily

STEP 3: Dosing guidance
  → Divide daily dose into 3 doses with meals
  → Take at least 2 hours apart from iron tablets
  → Start at Contact 1, continue throughout pregnancy
  → Common forms: calcium carbonate, calcium citrate
```

## 3. GDM Screening Decision Tree

```
STEP 1: Check gestational age
  IF GA 24-28 weeks (Contact 3 window):
    → Proceed to Step 2
  IF GA < 24 weeks:
    → Screen at Contact 3 (26 weeks) unless early screening indicated
    → Early screening if: prior GDM, BMI ≥ 30, strong family history
  IF GA > 28 weeks and NOT yet screened:
    → Screen now (late screening still valuable)

STEP 2: Identify risk factors
  HIGH risk (any ONE):
    - Previous GDM
    - Previous macrosomic infant (>4000g)
    - BMI ≥ 30
    - Strong family history (first-degree relative with DM)
    - Previous unexplained stillbirth
    - Polycystic ovarian syndrome

  MODERATE risk:
    - Age ≥ 25
    - BMI 25-29.9
    - Non-white ethnicity
    - Random glucose 7.0-7.8 mmol/L

STEP 3: Testing approach
  IF any HIGH risk factor:
    → 75g OGTT recommended
    → Fasting glucose + 1-hour + 2-hour readings
    → Diagnostic thresholds (WHO): Fasting ≥5.1, 1h ≥10.0, 2h ≥8.5 mmol/L
  IF ≥2 MODERATE risk factors:
    → 75g OGTT recommended
  IF low risk:
    → Random or fasting glucose at Contact 3
    → OGTT if random glucose ≥7.8 mmol/L or fasting ≥5.1

  IF OGTT unavailable (resource-limited):
    → Fasting glucose or random glucose as alternative
    → Refer if fasting ≥7.0 or random ≥11.1 (overt diabetes threshold)

STEP 4: If GDM diagnosed
  → Dietary counseling (first-line management)
  → Blood glucose self-monitoring if available
  → Insulin if dietary management fails (metformin as alternative where guidelines permit)
  → Increased fetal surveillance (growth scans)
  → Plan delivery at 38-39 weeks if well-controlled, earlier if poorly controlled
```

## 4. HIV Testing Decision Tree

```
STEP 1: Determine prevalence setting
  HIGH prevalence (>5% in pregnant women):
    → Opt-out testing at Contact 1 (all women tested unless they decline)
    → Retest at Contact 3 (third trimester) for those initially negative
    → Retest in labor if last test was >3 months ago
  LOW prevalence (<5%):
    → Opt-out testing at Contact 1
    → Retest based on individual risk assessment

STEP 2: If HIV-NEGATIVE at Contact 1
  IF high-prevalence setting:
    → Retest at Contact 3 (~26 weeks)
    → Counsel on prevention (condoms, PrEP if available and indicated)
  IF partner is HIV-positive or unknown status:
    → Offer partner testing
    → Counsel on prevention
    → Consider PrEP where available

STEP 3: If HIV-POSITIVE (new or known)
  → Confirm with second test (per national algorithm)
  → Initiate or continue ART: TDF/3TC/DTG (or per national first-line)
  → Viral load at baseline, at 3 months, then every 6 months
  → CD4 count if available (for OI prophylaxis decisions)
  → PMTCT counseling: adherence, infant feeding, infant prophylaxis
  → IF on cotrimoxazole: do NOT give IPTp-SP (contraindication)
  → Partner testing and disclosure support

STEP 4: Infant prophylaxis planning (before delivery)
  → Document infant ARV prophylaxis plan
  → High-risk infants: extended NVP regimen
  → Standard risk: NVP or AZT for 4-6 weeks
  → Infant HIV testing schedule
  → Infant feeding counseling (exclusive breastfeeding recommended even with HIV)
```

## 5. Syphilis Testing and Treatment

```
STEP 1: Screen at Contact 1
  → Rapid syphilis test (RPR or VDRL, confirmed with treponemal test)
  → Universal screening — do not risk-stratify

STEP 2: If POSITIVE
  → Benzathine penicillin G 2.4 million units IM single dose
  → Treat partner(s) concurrently
  → If penicillin allergy: desensitization + penicillin (NO substitute in pregnancy for syphilis)
  → Retest at 3 months post-treatment (serological response)
  → If late syphilis (>1 year or unknown duration): 3 weekly doses

STEP 3: If NEGATIVE at Contact 1
  → Rescreen in third trimester in high-prevalence settings
  → Screen at delivery if no third-trimester test

STEP 4: Congenital syphilis prevention
  → Adequate maternal treatment before 36 weeks prevents most congenital syphilis
  → Document treatment and response for delivery planning
  → Infant evaluation if: maternal treatment <4 weeks before delivery, inadequate maternal treatment, or rising titers
```

## 6. Tetanus Toxoid (TT) Vaccination Schedule

```
STEP 1: Assess vaccination history
  IF no prior TT/Td doses OR unknown history:
    → TT1 at first ANC contact
    → TT2 at least 4 weeks after TT1
    → TT3 at least 6 months after TT2 (may be postpartum or next pregnancy)
    → TT4 at least 1 year after TT3
    → TT5 at least 1 year after TT4
    → 5 doses = lifetime protection

  IF 1 prior dose:
    → TT2 now, then TT3 at 6 months
  IF 2 prior doses:
    → TT3 now if ≥6 months since TT2
  IF 3 prior doses:
    → TT4 now if ≥1 year since TT3
  IF 4 prior doses:
    → TT5 now if ≥1 year since TT4
  IF 5 doses completed:
    → Protected for life, no further doses needed

STEP 2: In current pregnancy
  → Give at least 2 doses during pregnancy (minimum 4 weeks apart)
  → First dose as early as possible
  → Second dose at least 2 weeks before expected delivery
  → Document clearly for future pregnancies
```
