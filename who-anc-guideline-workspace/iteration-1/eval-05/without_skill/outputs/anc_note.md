## 2. Clinical Assessments

### 2.1 Gestational Diabetes Screening — HIGH PRIORITY

**Risk classification: HIGH RISK for gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM)**

Multiple risk factors identified:
1. **Previous macrosomic infant** — prior delivery of 4.2 kg infant (macrosomia defined as birth weight >4.0 kg)
2. **First-degree family history of type 2 diabetes** — mother diagnosed with T2DM
3. **Overweight BMI** — 29.0 kg/m2 (approaching obese threshold of 30)
4. **Elevated random blood glucose** — 7.8 mmol/L with trace glycosuria on urine dipstick
5. **South Asian ethnicity** — higher baseline population risk for GDM and T2DM
6. **Dietary pattern** — frequent consumption of sweetened beverages and refined carbohydrates
7. **Sedentary lifestyle** — minimal structured physical activity

**Action required:** Order a **75g oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT)** within the next 1-2 weeks. The OGTT should include fasting, 1-hour, and 2-hour plasma glucose measurements.

**WHO 2013 diagnostic criteria for hyperglycemia in pregnancy:**

| Diagnosis | Fasting glucose | 1-hour glucose | 2-hour glucose |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|----------------|
| GDM | 5.1–6.9 mmol/L | >= 10.0 mmol/L | 8.5–11.0 mmol/L |
| Diabetes in pregnancy | >= 7.0 mmol/L | — | >= 11.1 mmol/L |

*Any one abnormal value is sufficient for diagnosis.*

Patient to be referred to the district hospital laboratory where OGTT can be performed.

### 4.4 Danger Signs Review
