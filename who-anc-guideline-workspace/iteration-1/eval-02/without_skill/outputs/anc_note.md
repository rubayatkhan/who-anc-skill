## 3. Supplementation Recommendations

### Iron Supplementation -- THERAPEUTIC DOSE
- **Prescription:** Elemental iron 120 mg daily (therapeutic dose)
- **Rationale:** WHO recommends therapeutic-dose iron (120 mg elemental iron daily) for pregnant women with moderate anemia in settings where anemia prevalence is >=40%
- **Duration:** Continue until hemoglobin normalizes (>=11 g/dL), then step down to standard prophylactic dose (30-60 mg elemental iron daily) for the remainder of pregnancy
- **Administration:** Take on an empty stomach or with vitamin C-rich foods to enhance absorption; avoid taking with tea, coffee, or calcium-rich foods

### Folic Acid
- **Prescription:** Folic acid 0.4 mg (400 mcg) daily
- **Rationale:** Standard WHO recommendation for all pregnant women for neural tube defect prevention and to support red blood cell production
- **Duration:** Throughout pregnancy

### Deworming
- **Prescription:** Single-dose albendazole 400 mg OR mebendazole 500 mg
- **Timing:** To be administered after the first trimester (at or after 13 weeks gestation -- schedule for next contact or at 13 weeks)
- **Rationale:** WHO recommends preventive deworming in areas where soil-transmitted helminth prevalence warrants it; patient has had no recent deworming treatment

## 5. Counseling Topics

### Dietary Counseling for Anemia
- **Increase iron-rich foods:** Dark leafy greens (spinach, moringa leaves), beans, lentils, fortified cereals; include animal-source foods (liver, red meat, fish) when available
- **Enhance iron absorption:** Consume vitamin C-rich foods with meals (citrus fruits, tomatoes, mangoes, guava)
- **Avoid absorption inhibitors:** Do not drink tea or coffee with meals or within 1 hour of iron supplementation; these contain tannins/polyphenols that reduce iron absorption
- **Meal frequency:** Encourage regular meals and snacks; address the "fair" appetite by eating small, frequent meals

### Danger Signs Counseling
Educate the patient to seek immediate care for:
- **Severe anemia signs:** Severe fatigue, breathlessness at rest, rapid heartbeat/palpitations, dizziness or fainting
- **General pregnancy danger signs:** Vaginal bleeding, severe headache, blurred vision, epigastric pain, reduced or absent fetal movements (later in pregnancy), fever, swelling of face/hands

### Side Effects of Iron Supplementation
- Common: nausea, constipation, dark stools
- Advise taking with food if gastrointestinal upset occurs (though absorption is better on an empty stomach)
- Dark stools are expected and not a cause for concern

### General ANC Counseling
- Importance of attending all scheduled ANC contacts
- Birth preparedness and complication readiness (begin discussion)
- Nutrition during pregnancy -- balanced diet, adequate protein intake
- Rest and activity -- continue normal activities but avoid overexertion given anemia
