## IMMEDIATE MANAGEMENT PLAN

### 1. Magnesium Sulfate — Seizure Prophylaxis (MANDATORY)
- **Loading dose:** MgSO4 4g IV over 15-20 minutes PLUS 5g IM in each buttock (10g IM total)
- **Maintenance:** 5g IM every 4 hours OR 1g/hour IV infusion for 24 hours
- **Monitoring for MgSO4 toxicity:**
  - Respiratory rate every 1 hour (withhold if RR <16/min)
  - Patellar reflexes every 1 hour (withhold if absent)
  - Urine output hourly (withhold if <30 mL/hour)
  - Keep calcium gluconate 1g at bedside as antidote

### 2. Antihypertensive Therapy — Urgent BP Reduction
- **Target:** Reduce BP to <150/100 mmHg but not below 130/80 mmHg
- **Options (any one, based on availability and institutional protocol):**
  - Nifedipine 10 mg oral (immediate release), repeat in 30 minutes if needed, OR
  - Hydralazine 5 mg IV slow bolus, repeat in 20 minutes if needed, OR
  - Labetalol 20 mg IV, double dose every 15 minutes up to 80 mg (max 300 mg total)
- **Monitor BP every 15 minutes** until stable, then every 30 minutes

### 3. Antenatal Corticosteroids — Fetal Lung Maturation

- 34 weeks GA with anticipated delivery within 24-48 hours

### 4. Supportive Measures

- **Urinary catheter:** Insert for strict hourly urine output monitoring (target >=30 mL/hour)
- **Position:** Left lateral position to optimize uteroplacental perfusion

### 5. Laboratory Investigations — URGENT

- Liver function tests: AST, ALT (>2x upper limit of normal indicates severe disease/HELLP)
- Serum creatinine (>1.1 mg/dL or doubling indicates renal involvement)
- Uric acid
- Coagulation studies: PT/INR (assess for DIC)
- Peripheral blood smear (if HELLP suspected — look for schistocytes)

### 6. Fetal Monitoring

### 8. Delivery Planning
