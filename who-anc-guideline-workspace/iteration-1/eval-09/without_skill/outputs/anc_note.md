## 2. Clinical Assessments and Risk Classification

- **Classification:** Moderate anemia (Hb 8.5 g/dL; WHO defines moderate anemia in pregnancy as Hb 7.0-9.9 g/dL)
- **Trend:** Progressive decline despite prescribed supplementation (10.8 -> 9.6 -> 8.5 g/dL over 14 weeks)
- **Contributing factors:**
  - Poor adherence to iron/folic acid supplementation (patient-reported: forgets doses, constipation side effects)
  - Dietary insufficiency: high carbohydrate intake, limited protein and vegetables, infrequent red meat consumption
  - Physiological hemodilution of pregnancy (expected but cannot explain the magnitude of decline)
- **Symptoms consistent with anemia:** Progressive fatigue, breathlessness on exertion, positional dizziness

### B. Pre-eclampsia Risk -- HIGH

- **Risk factors identified:**
  1. Nulliparity (first pregnancy)
  2. Obesity (BMI >= 30)
  3. Elevated blood pressure with upward trend (126/80 -> 128/82 -> 130/85)
  4. New and progressive proteinuria (negative -> trace -> +1)
  5. Family history of pre-eclampsia (sister, first pregnancy)
  6. Approaching advanced maternal age (34 years)
- **Current status:** Does NOT yet meet criteria for gestational hypertension (BP < 140/90) or pre-eclampsia, but the combination of rising BP, new proteinuria, and multiple risk factors places this patient in a high-surveillance category
- **Note:** Low-dose aspirin was NOT initiated at booking despite identifiable risk factors -- this was a missed opportunity

### C. Gestational Diabetes Risk -- ELEVATED

- **Risk factors:**
  1. Obesity (BMI >= 30)

  3. Age 34 years
- **Random blood glucose 6.2 mmol/L:** Non-fasting, not diagnostic, but warrants formal testing

## 3. Supplementation Recommendations

### A. Iron -- SWITCH TO THERAPEUTIC DOSING

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Regimen | 120 mg elemental iron daily (therapeutic dose) |
| Rationale | Moderate anemia (Hb 8.5 g/dL) with declining trend despite prophylactic dose (60 mg/day) |
| Administration | Take on an empty stomach or with vitamin C-rich food/drink to enhance absorption; avoid taking with tea, coffee, or calcium supplements (separate by at least 2 hours) |
| Side-effect management | For constipation: increase dietary fiber, adequate fluid intake; consider switching formulation (e.g., ferrous fumarate or liquid preparation) if constipation persists |
| Folic acid | Continue 400 mcg daily alongside therapeutic iron |
| Monitoring | Recheck hemoglobin in 4 weeks; if no improvement (rise of at least 1 g/dL), investigate further (complete blood count with indices, peripheral smear, serum ferritin if available, stool examination for parasites) |
