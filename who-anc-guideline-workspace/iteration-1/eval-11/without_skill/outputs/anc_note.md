## 6. Counseling Provided

### A. Malaria Prevention — ITN Use (Priority Counseling Topic)

- Reinforced the critical importance of sleeping under the ITN every night without exception
- Explained that Anopheles mosquitoes (malaria vectors) bite primarily between dusk and dawn, and even a single unprotected night carries infection risk in this high-transmission area
- Addressed the patient's concern about heat: discussed strategies such as ensuring the net is properly hung to allow airflow, sleeping in lighter clothing, and positioning the bed for ventilation
- Explained that malaria in pregnancy can cause severe anemia, low birth weight, preterm birth, and maternal death — even without obvious symptoms
- Discussed that IPTp-SP provides intermittent clearance of parasites but is NOT a substitute for consistent ITN use; both interventions together provide the best protection

### B. Nutrition and Anemia Management

- Explained that the hemoglobin drop (11.0 to 10.2 g/dL) indicates mild anemia, which is common but requires attention
- Dietary counseling provided:
  - Increase intake of iron-rich foods: dark green leafy vegetables (sukuma wiki is good; encourage increased portions), beans, lentils, and animal-source foods (liver, eggs, small fish like omena) when accessible and affordable
  - Consume iron-rich foods with vitamin C sources (oranges, tomatoes, lemon) to enhance absorption
  - Avoid taking iron supplements with tea or coffee (inhibits absorption)
  - Encouraged increasing from two to three meals daily if possible, with protein at each meal
- Reinforced importance of daily iron/folic acid tablet adherence
- Explained that iron tablets may cause dark stools (normal) and to take with food if nausea occurs

### C. Danger Signs Review

Counseled patient to seek care immediately if she experiences any of the following:

**Malaria-related danger signs:**
- Fever, chills, or rigors
- Severe headache not relieved by paracetamol
- Body aches with high temperature

**Pregnancy-related danger signs:**
- Vaginal bleeding (any amount)
- Severe headache with visual disturbances (blurred vision, flashing lights)
- Epigastric or right upper quadrant pain
- Sudden swelling of face, hands, or feet
- Decreased or absent fetal movement
- Leaking fluid from vagina (premature rupture of membranes)
- Convulsions
- Persistent vomiting
- Severe abdominal pain
- High fever

Instructed patient on where to seek emergency care (sub-county hospital, 18 km away) and to arrange transport promptly.
