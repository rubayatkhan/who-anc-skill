## 4. Catch-Up Supplementation and Preventive Interventions

### 4.1 Iron and Folic Acid
- **Status:** None received this pregnancy
- **Hb:** 11.5 g/dL (not anemic)
- **Action:** Initiate daily oral iron (30-60 mg elemental iron) and folic acid (0.4 mg) supplementation today
- **Rationale:** WHO recommends daily iron and folic acid throughout pregnancy to prevent anemia (WHO ANC Recommendation A.3). Continue through remainder of pregnancy.
- **Dispense:** Supply for 4 weeks; replenish at next contact

### 4.2 IPTp-SP (Intermittent Preventive Treatment for Malaria)
- **Status:** No doses received; patient is well beyond 13 weeks GA
- **Action:** Administer first dose of sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine (SP) today as directly observed therapy (DOT)
- **Schedule catch-up:**
  - Dose 1: Today (32 weeks)
  - Dose 2: At 34 weeks (next ANC contact; minimum 1 month apart)
  - Dose 3: At 38 weeks if feasible
- **Rationale:** WHO recommends IPTp-SP at each ANC contact from 13 weeks onwards in malaria-endemic areas, with doses at least 1 month apart. Aim for at least 3 doses. (WHO ANC Recommendation A.6)
- **Note:** Malaria RDT is negative today; SP can still be given as preventive therapy

### 4.3 Tetanus Toxoid
- **Status:** TT1 and TT2 received approximately 5 years ago during a previous pregnancy
- **Action:** Administer TT3 booster today
- **Rationale:** Per WHO schedule, TT3 is due at least 1 year after TT2, providing protection for at least 5 more years. The patient is eligible and due for TT3. (WHO ANC Recommendation A.8)

### 4.4 Calcium Supplementation
- **Status:** Not received this pregnancy

- **Rationale:** WHO recommends calcium supplementation in populations with low dietary calcium intake to reduce the risk of pre-eclampsia (WHO ANC Recommendation A.4)

### 4.5 Deworming (Mebendazole)

## 5. Counseling Topics for This Visit

### 5.1 Birth Preparedness and Complication Readiness (HIGH PRIORITY)
- **Current plan:** Patient intends to deliver at home with TBA (as with prior births)
- **Counsel strongly on:**
  - Benefits of facility-based delivery with a skilled birth attendant
  - Identify the nearest facility for delivery (health post or district hospital)
  - Establish a transport plan: how to get to the facility (vehicle, phone number of driver, cost)
  - Identify a birth companion
  - Set aside funds for emergency transport and care
  - Identify compatible blood donors among family/community in case of hemorrhage
  - Pack a delivery kit: clean cloths, razor blade, cord ties, sanitary pads
  - Discuss that grand multiparity carries increased risk of postpartum hemorrhage

### 5.2 Danger Signs Requiring Immediate Care
Counsel patient (and birth companion/family) to seek care immediately if any of the following occur:
- Vaginal bleeding
- Severe headache or blurred vision
- Epigastric or upper abdominal pain
- Sudden swelling of face or hands
- Convulsions or loss of consciousness
- Fever or chills
- Reduced or absent fetal movements
- Leaking fluid from the vagina (premature rupture of membranes)
- Severe abdominal pain
- Difficulty breathing

### 5.3 Nutrition Counseling
- Encourage diverse diet with increased protein, iron-rich foods (dark leafy greens, beans, animal-source foods if available)
- Adequate hydration
- Importance of taking supplements daily as prescribed
- Timing of calcium and iron doses (separate by at least 2 hours)

### 5.4 Breastfeeding Preparation
- Discuss plans for breastfeeding; patient has successfully breastfed prior children
- Encourage exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months after delivery
- Note: patient is currently weaning P3 — confirm weaning is progressing

### 5.5 Family Planning Counseling
- Current method: Breastfeeding and periodic abstinence (not reliable modern contraception)
- Discuss postpartum family planning options to be initiated after delivery
- Provide information on LARC methods (IUD, implant) appropriate for this setting
- Discuss timing of postpartum family planning initiation

### 5.6 HIV Testing Counseling
