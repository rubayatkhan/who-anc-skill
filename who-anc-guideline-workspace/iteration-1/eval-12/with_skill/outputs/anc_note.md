- Symptoms: **None** — no headache, no visual disturbances, no epigastric or right upper quadrant pain, no nausea/vomiting

**Diagnostic reasoning:**
1. **New-onset hypertension after 20 weeks:** All documented BP readings prior to 20 weeks were normotensive (112/68 at 12 weeks, 118/72 at 20 weeks, 115/70 at 26 weeks, 120/74 at 30 weeks). This excludes chronic hypertension.
2. **Absence of proteinuria:** Urine dipstick is protein-negative today, which differentiates this from pre-eclampsia. Pre-eclampsia requires hypertension PLUS proteinuria (>=+1 dipstick or >=300 mg/24h) OR end-organ dysfunction.
3. **Absence of severe features:** No headache, no visual changes, no epigastric pain, no evidence of thrombocytopenia, renal insufficiency, or elevated liver enzymes. BP is <160/110 mmHg. This rules out pre-eclampsia with severe features.
4. **Not eclampsia:** No seizures or altered mental status.
5. **Not superimposed pre-eclampsia:** Patient has no history of chronic hypertension.

**Key concern: Risk of progression.** Gestational hypertension can progress to pre-eclampsia. Serial monitoring of BP and proteinuria is essential.

Decision should be individualized based on clinical judgment.
