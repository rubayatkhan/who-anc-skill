## 4. Counseling Topics

### Pre-eclampsia Awareness (Priority)
- Educated patient on warning signs requiring immediate medical attention:
  - Severe headache (persistent, not relieved by simple analgesics)
  - Visual disturbances (blurred vision, seeing spots or flashing lights)
  - Epigastric or right upper quadrant pain
  - Sudden swelling of face, hands, or feet
  - Decreased urine output
  - Sudden rapid weight gain
- Advised to present to facility immediately if any of these symptoms develop
- Explained the importance of daily aspirin and calcium adherence for risk reduction

### Blood Pressure Management
- Importance of continuing methyldopa as prescribed
- Encouraged continued home BP monitoring (current range 125-140 / 80-90 noted)
- Target: maintain BP below 140/90 mmHg
- Report any readings consistently above 140/90 or any sudden BP spike
