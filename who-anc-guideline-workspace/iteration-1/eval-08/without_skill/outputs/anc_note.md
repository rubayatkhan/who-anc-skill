### 5.2 Danger Signs Review
Reinforced the following signs that require immediate presentation to the health facility:
- Severe headache that does not resolve with rest
- Visual disturbances (blurred vision, seeing spots)
- Epigastric pain or pain under the ribs
- Vaginal bleeding (any amount)
- Sudden swelling of the face, hands, or feet
- Decreased or absent fetal movement
- Fever or chills
- Gush of fluid from the vagina (possible rupture of membranes)
- Regular, painful contractions before 37 weeks
- Convulsions

### 5.3 Breastfeeding Counseling

### 5.5 Nutritional Counseling

### 5.6 Newborn Care Preparation
