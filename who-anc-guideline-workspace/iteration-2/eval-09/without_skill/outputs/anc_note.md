## Assessment

### 1. Anemia
The patient has anemia with Hb of 8.5 g/dL, which has been progressively declining (10.8 -> 9.6 -> 8.5). She reports poor adherence to iron supplementation due to constipation. This degree of anemia is concerning and requires increased iron supplementation. Consider increasing her iron dose and counseling on adherence. A complete blood count and peripheral smear may be helpful to rule out other causes.

### 2. Blood Pressure
BP is 130/85 which is in the high-normal range. While not meeting criteria for hypertension (140/90), the upward trend from 126/80 to 130/85 is concerning, especially in the context of proteinuria. Monitor closely.

### 3. Proteinuria
New finding of +1 protein on dipstick, progressing from negative to trace to +1. This needs monitoring. If BP rises above 140/90, this could indicate pre-eclampsia. Consider quantifying with a spot protein:creatinine ratio.

### 4. Obesity
BMI 32 (obese). Associated with higher risk of gestational diabetes, pre-eclampsia, and delivery complications. Dietary counseling recommended.

### 5. GDM Screening
Given obesity and family history of diabetes, glucose tolerance testing should be arranged. The random glucose of 6.2 is not diagnostic. An oral glucose tolerance test should be performed.

### 6. Surgical History
Prior myomectomy with cavity entry 3 years ago. This is relevant for delivery planning -- the patient may need a planned cesarean section. Obstetric consultation recommended.

## Plan

1. **Iron supplementation:** Increase dose for anemia. Counsel on adherence, taking with vitamin C, and managing constipation.
2. **Continue folic acid** 400mcg daily
3. **Blood pressure monitoring:** Recheck in 2 weeks given rising trend and proteinuria
4. **Urine monitoring:** Repeat dipstick at next visit; consider quantitative protein testing
5. **Order OGTT** for GDM screening (24-28 weeks)
6. **Dietary counseling:** Improve iron-rich food intake, reduce excessive carbohydrates
7. **Obstetrician referral** for delivery planning given prior myomectomy
8. **Danger sign counseling:** Advise to seek immediate care for headache, visual changes, sudden swelling, decreased fetal movement
9. **Hospital delivery planned** given risk factors
10. **Follow-up in 2 weeks** for BP and proteinuria recheck
