## Clinical Assessment

The patient is a 30-year-old G2P1 at 26 weeks gestation presenting for routine antenatal care. Her vitals are stable with normal blood pressure. However, several findings warrant attention:

1. **Elevated random blood glucose (7.8 mmol/L)** with trace glycosuria — this is above normal and suggests possible impaired glucose tolerance or gestational diabetes.

2. **Risk factors for gestational diabetes:**
   - Previous large baby (4.2 kg — macrosomia)
   - Family history of type 2 diabetes (mother)
   - Overweight BMI (29.0)
   - Diet high in refined carbohydrates and sweetened beverages
   - Sedentary lifestyle

3. **Blood pressure and pre-eclampsia risk** appear low at present. Normal BP, no proteinuria.

## Plan

### Blood Glucose Follow-Up
- Given the elevated random glucose and multiple risk factors, further glucose testing is recommended
- A glucose tolerance test should be arranged to formally evaluate for gestational diabetes
- If diabetes is confirmed, dietary management would be the first step, with possible medication if needed

### Dietary Advice
- Counsel on reducing sugar-sweetened beverages and refined carbohydrate intake
- Encourage a balanced diet with adequate fiber, protein, and vegetables
- Regular meal times to avoid blood sugar spikes

### Exercise
- Encourage moderate physical activity such as daily walking (30 minutes)
- Currently sedentary — gradual increase in activity recommended

### Medications
- Continue iron and folic acid supplementation as prescribed

### Follow-Up
- Return for next visit in approximately 4 weeks
- Complete glucose testing before next appointment
- Monitor fetal growth — given history of macrosomia and current glucose concerns, ultrasound for fetal weight estimation may be warranted later in pregnancy

### Warning Signs
- Advise patient to report: excessive thirst, frequent urination, blurred vision, headaches, decreased fetal movement, vaginal bleeding, or sudden swelling
